About Us

Founded in 1947, the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) is the world’s largest scientific and education computing society. Our school chapter UNLV ACM works to connect student by hosting workshops, lectures, attending seminars, and competing in regional coding competitions. We learn from professionals in the industry, gain coding experience, and land internships! Anyone with a passion for coding or for Computer Science are welcome to join!

What We Do

ICPC

Every November, UNLV ACM members compete in the International Collegiate Programming Competition (ICPC) Southern California Regional Contest held in Riverside, CA. There students have a chance to show off their coding skills and gain competition experience with other university students.

Coding Workshops

We let students work on their programming skills by training them how to code in other languages. These workshops will teach you how to make impressive projects like web apps or native games!

Our Meetups


Come to our ICPC Coding Meetups! We meet up, discuss coding problems,
and prep for ICPC (our coding competition in California) every Saturday.

ICPC

Every November we compete in the International Collegiate Programming Competition held in Riverside, California. This lets students sharpen their problem solving and coding skills.

This is what you can do to prepare for the competition:

  • Learn how to use Vim or Emacs
  • Have a solid foundation of the functioning and syntax of C++, Python, or Java
  • Try and solve past ICPC problems that can be found here

Coding Workshops

It can be challenging trying to learn a new programming language on your own. But there is no need to worry, that is why we are here! During the semester we will have workshops that will help you learn new languages.

Workshops will include the following:

  • Python
  • JavaScript
  • Android
  • Unity
  • Git
